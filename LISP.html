LISP

This page has details about uses, and the history and evolution of LISP.

What is LISP Used For?

LISP was designed for easy manipulation of data strings, and is relatively simple. It is used as a general purpose scripting language, and it is also used for artificial intelligence research.

General History and Evolution of LISP.

LISP was created in 1959 by by John McCarthy from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The first implementation of Lisp was used on an IBM 704 using punched cards.

LISP in the 1960s and 70s

The popularity of LISP grew throughout the 1960s and was used by researchers and developers for artificial intelligence research. In the 1970s, a new dialect of LISP called Scheme was created, which was meant to be a more simple version of LISP used to teach computer science courses.
